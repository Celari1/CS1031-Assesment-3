The town's name, Dunblane, is derived from the word 'dun' which means fortified hill and the word 'Blane' who was a St who came to the town in the 7th century. Dunblane can claim a city status from its large cathedral, but in reality it is still very much a town.

The town of Dunblane is a small, charming and interesting town that is located at the entrance to the Scottish Hghlands. The settlement had been located there for centuries but the town has almost tripled in size since the late 20th century and the early 21st century.

The town also features the Allan Water river which runs directly through town center. The river eventually flows into the larger River Forth, this river also becomes the Firth of Forth. This river is what brought people to the town, mills and weaving provided work for the people in town.
