Local Attractions


Dunblane Cathedral

  • The cathedral has been the focal part of Dunblane for centuries. Is one of Scotland's few surviving medieval churches and when viewed from the outside, the Cathedral looks impressive, but its true beauty lies within.

Dunblane Library

  • The Dunblane Library and the Church hall next door were formerly known as the Dunblane Institute and Church Hall. It was built on the old vegetable garden of Balhaldie House.

Dunblane Museum

  • The Dunblane Museum houses a selection of artifacts, paintings, prints and photographs about the Cathedral and Dunblane. It also has one of the largest collections of Communion tokens. The Museum is located at The Cross in Dunblane, across the road from Dunblane Cathedral.

False Chapel on Smithy Loan

  • The chapel like structure was built using the remains of building stones and arcitectural fatures left after the Red Comyn Inn was demolished. It looks like a real restored building but is really just the salvaged bits probably taken from older buildings around the town.

Golden Postbox

  • Dunblane has its own golden post box to celebrate our gold medal winner Andy Murray. Andy won the gold in the mens singles tennis in London in 2012 and again in Rio in 2016. The postbox is found at the roundabout on High Street close to the Cathedral.

Holmehill

  • Holmehill is a 13 acre greenspace in the centre of Dunblane. Positioned to the east of the River Allan, Holmehill is a prominent, attractive, and important landscape feature. Although now well wooded with many mature trees, the origin of the name Holmehill,'The Hollow of the Bare Hillâ€™, suggestests a time in history when it may have looked very different.

Laigh Hills Park

  • The Laigh Hills Park is a large, fairly natural area that is easily accessed from the Cathedral and the centre of Dunblane. In the park there is a play area at the south end of the Laigh Hills and this is the first part you come see. The play equipment at the site has revamped in 2018. There is also a sand play area with rope climber for young children.

Leighton Library

  • The Leighton Library is the oldest purpose-built library in Scotland. It was founded on the collection of Robert Leighton, Bishop of Dunblane, 1661-1670. In his will, Leighton left a sum of money for the building of the library. The library is found close to the Dunblane Cathedral.

Ochlochy Park

  • The Park was once a common place to let cattle graze, It was then turned into a children's play area in 1942. It is a pleasant place for a short walk with its the streams, bridges and ponds. The pond itself in its present form was buit by Victorian engineers as a water reservoir for the railway.

Sheriffmuir

  • The scene of the battle of Sheriffmuir 1715 can be observed by the obelisk monument to the Clan Macrae erected in 1915. A small walk from the monument is the Gathering Stone. The Gathering Stone is a block of grit, enclosed in an iron cage since 1840, where the standard of the Scottish clans is said to have been placed. If you carry along this road you will arrive at the Sheriffmuir Inn, a child-friendly hotel which is a lovely spot for lunch or dinner.

Allan Water and The Haugh

  • Walk along the riverbank of the river Allan which runs through the middle of town. Paths can be accessed from the cathedral or either end of the bridge in the middle of the town centre.

Burgh Chambers : Stirling Council's Local Office

  • Stirling Council's Local Office is rooted in the Burgh Chambers close to the Cathedral. The building is featured in the Kenneth More version of The Thirty Nine Steps (1959) film. Where they leapt from the window of the local Sheriff's office. This was actually from an upstairs room at the Burgh Chambers.

David Stirling Monument

  • David Stirling is known as the founder of the SAS. In 2002 a memorial statue was erected for him on the Hill of Row, near his family's estate at Park of Keir, Dunblane.
